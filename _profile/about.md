I love to be a force that drives change — motivated to move things forward, improve what exists, and bring a touch of novelty that builds meaningfully on the previous iteration. I am also a keen learner: I want to absorb as much knowledge and motivation as I can from the people around me, not only from their work, but from their experiences and stories.

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Arlington, where I research live programming and interactive feedback for finite model finders. My work sits at the intersection of formal methods and human-computer interaction — specifically, I am interested in how we can make formal specification languages like Alloy more accessible and usable for novice users through intelligent tooling, live feedback, and AI-assisted completion.

My research interests span **Interactive & Live Programming**, **Model-Driven Engineering**, **Formal Methods**, **NLP-assisted Program Synthesis**, and the **Usability of Model Finders**.

Before starting my PhD, I spent over seven years as a software engineer building large-scale distributed systems and backend services in industry — at Agoda in Bangkok, Priyo Sys Ltd. in Dhaka, and iPay Systems Ltd. in Dhaka. That experience gave me deep practical knowledge of AWS, Kafka, Elasticsearch, PostgreSQL, Python, Java, and microservices architecture. I bring that engineering background into my research, which tends to be systems-oriented and tool-focused rather than purely theoretical.
