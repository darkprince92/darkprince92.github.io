Agoda is one of Asia's largest online travel agencies, operating at a scale of tens of millions of users daily. I joined the platform as a Software Engineer working on user account management and authentication infrastructure — a critical domain given the volume of global login events the platform processes around the clock.

My primary work was engineering backend features for the authentication system, serving end-users, B2B clients, and third-party integrations through a unified authentication layer. This involved maintaining and extending high-throughput services built on Java microservices and deployed on AWS.

A significant project I undertook was investigating and responding to login breach patterns. I analyzed login event streams to identify statistical anomalies indicative of credential stuffing and brute-force attacks. Based on that analysis, I designed and implemented a two-factor authentication solution and validated its effectiveness through targeted A/B testing before a full production rollout.

I also improved the monitoring and analytics pipelines using the ELK stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) to provide real-time visibility into login attack patterns across segregated security zones, allowing the security team to detect and respond to threats faster.

Working at Agoda sharpened my understanding of operating backend services at massive scale, engineering for high availability, and the practical considerations of deploying security-sensitive features in a globally distributed system.
